**✗ H1**  · for hierarchical models with **~one random effect per observation** (effective params ≈ N)
→ **exact LOO** (leave-one-observation-out) does **NOT** work to assess adding a covariate.
- why: each left-out point's own RE predicts it almost regardless of the covariate → LOO barely changes; PSIS-LOO is also unreliable (high Pareto k).
- conditions: per-observation REs (OLRE, network meta-analysis, few obs/subject); fine when groups have many observations each.
- tier: 🟡 · source: [mc-stan:12335](https://discourse.mc-stan.org/t/information-criteria-conflicting-advice-for-hierarchical-models/12335), [mc-stan:30254](https://discourse.mc-stan.org/t/binomial-glmm-assumptions/30254), [mc-stan:36639](https://discourse.mc-stan.org/t/interpreting-posterior-predictive-checks-on-a-longitudinal-beta-binomial-model-of-amphetamine-use/36639)

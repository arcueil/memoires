**✓ S2**  · for a **large smooth stationary GP** → a **Hilbert-space basis-function (HSGP) low-rank
approximation** works.
- why: replaces the dense GP with a structured low-rank eigenfunction expansion, cutting the per-gradient cost.
- conditions: smooth stationary kernel, inputs roughly centered/bounded; the eigenfunction approximation degrades near domain boundaries and for very small length scales.

## Technique (pymc-labs)

**HSGP m/c/L selection thresholds + approx_hsgp_hyperparams helper + the m-c coupling** — Actionable numbers for S2's vague 'degrades near boundaries and for very small length scales': c tables (interp 1.2-1.5; modest extrap 1.5-2.0; significant 2.0-4.0; c<1.2 pinches basis to zero at edges), m tables (very smooth 10-20; moderate 20-40; wiggly 40-80; highly variable 80+), the key insight that raising c REQUIRES raising m to hold approximation quality, and pm.gp.hsgp_approx.approx_hsgp_hyperparams(x_range, lengthscale_range, cov_func) as a starting-point heuristic (its outputs are minimums, verify with prior predictive).
*Source: [pymc-labs:gaussian-processes](https://github.com/pymc-labs/python-analytics-skills)*

**HSGP approximation-quality diagnostics: Gram-matrix comparison, subsampled exact-GP ground truth, prior-predictive of HSGP function draws** — Concrete PyMC 'how' for the S2 move 'monitor reconstruction error of the kernel online': K_true = cov(X).eval(); phi, sqrt_psd = gp.prior_linearized(X); K_approx = phi @ diag(sqrt_psd**2) @ phi.T; imshow both. Plus: fit an exact pm.gp.Marginal on a random subset (<=300 pts) as ground truth and compare hyperparameter posteriors. prior_linearized also exposes phi/sqrt_psd for custom linearized models.
*Source: [pymc-labs:gaussian-processes](https://github.com/pymc-labs/python-analytics-skills)*

**Tooling: nutpie NUTS backend ~2-5x faster on HSGP models; scale amplitude prior to outcome (HalfNormal(2*y_std))** — pm.sample(nuts_sampler='nutpie') gives ~2-5x speedup on HSGP models (relevant to the ess_per_sec slots), and a concrete weakly-informative amplitude prior scaled to data: eta = HalfNormal(2*y.std()).
*Source: [pymc-labs:gaussian-processes](https://github.com/pymc-labs/python-analytics-skills)*

**✗ G6**  · when a held-out unit has **Pareto-k > 0.7** → treating it as a **sampler / convergence
problem** does **NOT** work.
- why: k measures whether the leave-one-out posterior is close enough to the full-data posterior for importance sampling to bridge them; k>0.7 flags a highly *influential* held-out unit, not mixing and not (by itself) misspecification.
- conditions: PSIS-LOO/WAIC via loo/arviz; acute for hierarchical/latent-variable models and near-one-param-per-obs.
- tier: 🟡 · source: [mc-stan:3446](https://discourse.mc-stan.org/t/a-quick-note-what-i-infer-from-p-loo-and-pareto-k-values/3446), [mc-stan:22048](https://discourse.mc-stan.org/t/bad-pareto-k-diagnostic-with-good-chain-diagnostics/22048), [pymc:8256](https://discourse.pymc.io/t/need-help-for-calculating-loo-cv-with-multiple-observed-values/8256)

# C7 · Time-varying volatility is a first-class modeling target — give the conditional variance its own dynamics, don't assume a constant scale 🟢

**↑ [SA1 · The model is the object of inference; computation only certi](../../SUPER_AXIOMS.md)** · [Time-series & state-space models](../../pages/time-series-state-space.md)

**Statement.** When a returns-like series exhibits conditional heteroskedasticity (volatility clustering — large moves followed by large moves), the load-bearing modeling choice lives in the second moment: let the conditional variance follow its own deterministic recursion driven by past squared shocks and past variance (canonically GARCH(1,1): σ²_t = ω + α·ε²_{t−1} + β·σ²_{t−1}) rather than assuming a constant observation scale. The structure must match the data's generative feature (volatility clustering), not the surface 'returns' label.

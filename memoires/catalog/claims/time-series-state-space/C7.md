# C7 · Time-varying volatility is a first-class modeling target — give the conditional variance its own dynamics, don't assume a constant scale 🟡

**↑ [SA1 · The model is the object of inference; computation only certi](../../SUPER_AXIOMS.md)** · [Time-series & state-space models](../../pages/time-series-state-space.md)

**Statement.** When a returns-like series exhibits conditional heteroskedasticity (volatility clustering — large moves followed by large moves), the load-bearing modeling choice lives in the second moment: give the conditional variance its own dynamics rather than assuming a constant observation scale. Two constructions embody **different generative commitments**: an *observation-driven* deterministic recursion (GARCH(1,1): σ²_t = ω + α·ε²_{t−1} + β·σ²_{t−1}), appropriate **only when the noise level genuinely correlates with the realized signal/shock level**; or a *parameter-driven latent volatility process* (stochastic volatility: the variance follows its own time-dependent process with independent innovations, factoring the volatility dynamics away from the previous step's realized noise). Defaulting to GARCH is itself a structural claim — when volatility is better described as its own latent process, model it as one.
